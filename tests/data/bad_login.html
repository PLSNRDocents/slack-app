Docent Campfire Stories

This December 7th, cozy up to the campfire and listen to the varied and exceptional experiences that have unfolded in Point Lobos Reserve, as told by your fellow docents.\xc2\xa0 Some heartwarming, some funny some provoking, and always entertaining.\xc2\xa0 These are short vignettes in an informal and interactive setting where they will share a special experience they\xe2\x80\x99ve had with nature, with visitors or with each other.\xc2\xa0 So get comfy, pull up a chair and celebrate this special time of year, as we reflect on how important and fulfilling it is to be part of Point Lobos and reaffirm that this is why we volunteer. \xc2\xa0The stories are real, the campfire is not.

Mission Statements

CALIFORNIA STATE PARKS

\n

To provide for the health, inspiration and education of the people of California by helping to preserve the state\xe2\x80\x99s extraordinary biological diversity, protecting its most valued natural and cultural resources, and creating opportunities for high-quality outdoor recreation.

\n

Docent volunteers serve as a visible representative of California State Parks at Point Lobos State Natural Reserve promoting the California State Parks mission. \xc2\xa0These four simple words -- preserve, protect, educate and interpret -- sum up the docent experience.\xc2\xa0

\n

POINT LOBOS FOUNDATION

\n

Our mission is to protect and nurture Point Lobos State Natural Reserve,\xc2\xa0to educate and inspire visitors to preserve its unique natural and cultural\xc2\xa0resources, and to strengthen the network of Carmel Area State Parks.
